About WePOWER

In a world where energy and power dynamics significantly influence our daily existence, a pioneering initiative is poised to reshape the prevailing narrative. The "South Asia Gender and Energy" (SAGE) program, spearheaded by the World Bank, stands as a transformative endeavor. Central to this program is the visionary undertaking known as the "Women in Power Sector Network in South Asia (WePOWER)." This initiative serves as a beacon of change, aiming to reconfigure the landscape for women's participation in Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM) education. WePOWER doesn't merely aspire for change; it is a catalyst for a transformative shift in how society perceives and embraces women in the energy and power sector. Imagine a future where the clinking of hard hats and the hum of machinery are harmonized with the brilliance of women engineers and professionals, actively driving innovation and sustainability in the power domain. The WePOWER track is designed to encourage women's participation in power and energy fields, driving innovation and contributing to a cleaner approach to energy generation.

Theme

WePOWER Track 2025: Innovations for Clean, Affordable and Inclusive Energy Solutions

“Fostering Sustainable Energy Solutions for a Cleaner, Affordable, and Inclusive Future”

This theme focuses on reducing carbon footprints while ensuring energy availability for future generations. It promotes innovation in clean, affordable, and sustainable energy, with key focus areas including clean energy technologies, affordability, inclusivity, policy and governance, digital transformation, and community-driven solutions.

Key Focus Areas

  • Clean Energy Technologies: Innovations in renewable energy sources such as solar, wind, hydro, and bioenergy, along with advancements in energy storage and grid technologies.
  • Affordability and Accessibility: Strategies to reduce the cost of energy production and distribution, ensuring energy reaches underserved and remote communities.
  • Inclusivity and Gender Equality: Empowering women-led initiatives and fostering inclusive participation in the energy sector.
  • Policy and Governance: Frameworks and regulations that promote sustainable energy practices and investment.
  • Digital Transformation: The role of smart grids, IoT, AI, and blockchain in revolutionizing energy systems.
  • Community-Centric Solutions: Grassroots innovations that address local energy challenges and create sustainable impact.

Objective

WePOWER aims to actively promote the adoption of clean energy systems, envisioning a future where they not only contribute to a safe environment for future generations but also position the renewable energy sector as an appealing and rewarding career option for women. Beyond individual empowerment, WePOWER is dedicated to actively contributing to the comprehensive development of inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable cities and human settlements. This commitment involves fostering positive environmental and societal changes by the increased participation of women in leadership roles within the power and energy sectors. To enhance efficiency, WePOWER will focus on targeted initiatives, partnerships, and advocacy efforts that advance the dual goals of clean energy promotion and women's leadership in sustainable development.

Who Can Participate?

Participants in the WePOWER Track 2025 are diverse and inclusive, encompassing a wide array of individuals who align with the theme of "Clean and Affordable Energy."

The team size can have a maximum of 4 members of which 1 member must be a girl student.

The platform welcomes:

  • Innovators and Creators: Those who actively contribute to the development and promotion of sustainable energy solutions.
  • University Students: Students from various universities are eligible to participate, providing a platform for the next generation of innovators to showcase their talents and ideas.
  • Graduate Members: Individuals with graduate-level expertise who can offer solutions to the problem statements posed by the WePOWER Track. Their knowledge and skills contribute to the advancement of clean and affordable energy initiatives.

By bringing together this diverse range of participants, the WePOWER Track aims to create a collaborative environment where different perspectives and expertise converge to drive innovation in sustainable energy.

WHAT ARE THE PROJECTS CAN BE SUBMITTED?

The WePOWER track invites the participants to submit projects related to the following topics:

Innovative Solutions for Energy Storage Efficiency

  • Hybrid Energy Storage Systems: Combining batteries and supercapacitors.
  • Solid-State Batteries: Research and implementation for enhanced energy storage.
  • Compressed Air Energy Storage: Small-scale, cost-effective systems.
  • Repurposed EV Batteries for Grids: Retrofitting retired EV batteries for renewable storage.
  • AI-Based Storage Optimization: Algorithms to maximize energy performance.
  • Hydrogen Fuel Cells: Efficient storage for surplus renewable energy.
  • Gravity-Based Energy Storage: Solutions leveraging mechanical energy for storage.

Digital Technologies (IoT and AI) for Energy Optimization

  • IoT which helps in utilizing clean and affordable energy.
  • AI-Driven Load Forecasting: Predicting energy demand for better resource allocation.
  • IoT for Microgrid Management: Efficient real-time monitoring of distributed energy.
  • AI for Energy Theft Detection: Identifying inefficiencies or theft in underserved areas.
  • Renewable Energy Forecasting: AI for better prediction of solar and wind generation.
  • Virtual Power Plants: Coordination of distributed energy resources using AI.
  • Digital Twins for Energy Infrastructure: Simulation of energy systems for optimization.
  • AI-Powered Energy Distribution in Urban Slums: Addressing underserved areas effectively.

Community-Driven Renewable Energy Initiatives

  • Solar energy technologies made affordable and accessible to remote communities.
  • Cooperative Solar Farms: Community ownership of renewable resources.
  • Renewable Energy Crowdfunding: Platforms for raising funds for community projects.
  • Energy Literacy and Training Programs: Educating communities on renewable benefits.
  • Shared Solar Grids: Models for equitable use of shared energy resources.
  • Community Biogas Plants: Small-scale biogas projects for rural communities.
  • Rural Renewable Energy Mentorship Programs: Empowering communities.
  • Agricultural Renewable Energy Cooperatives: Tailored energy solutions for farmers.
  • Renewable Energy in Daily Life Applications.

Blockchain for Energy Transactions

  • Smart Energy Contracts: Automating transactions using blockchain technology.
  • Tokenized Energy Credits: Incentives for renewable energy producers and consumers.
  • Peer-to-Peer Energy Markets: Blockchain-based platforms for buying/selling energy.
  • Renewable Energy Certificate Verification: Transparent certification processes.
  • Carbon Offset Tracking: Blockchain for tracking carbon reductions.
  • Decentralized Energy Billing Systems: Transparent and tamper-proof models.
  • Supply Chain Transparency: Ensuring sustainability in energy equipment sourcing.

FAQs:

1. Who can participate in WePOWER?
The WePOWER track is open to high school students aged 12–17 years who are interested in renewable energy, sustainability, and innovation.
2. How can I submit my project?
Participants can submit their abstracts through Pilots or via Direct Entry. The submission link will be provided on the official platform.
3. What are the stages of the competition?
The competition includes two main stages — Stage 1: Abstract evaluation and Prelims, followed by Stage 2: Grand Finale for shortlisted teams.
4. Can I participate individually or with a team?
Participants can form teams of up to four members. Team members can be from different schools or academic institutions.
5. What kind of projects can be submitted?
Projects focusing on energy storage efficiency, AI and IoT for energy optimization, community-driven renewable energy, or blockchain in the energy sector are encouraged.
6. Are hardware projects mandatory?
No, participants can submit either software-based or hardware-integrated solutions depending on their idea and approach.
7. How will the evaluation be done?
Evaluation will be based on innovation, feasibility, technical approach, sustainability, scalability, and presentation clarity.
8. Is plagiarism allowed?
No. Any form of plagiarism or copied work will lead to immediate disqualification at any stage.
9. What happens after team selection?
Selected teams must submit an executive summary and a short video demonstration of their project before the final presentation.
