About Junior Einstein

Ever wondered how a single idea can change the world? The Junior Einstein Track is not just a competition; it's a journey that ignites the curiosity and imagination of young minds. This track is dedicated to empowering the next generation of innovators, nurturing their ideas, and fueling their passion for finding solutions to various social issues. We endeavor to channel the creative submissions and innovative spirit of school students aged 12 to 17 towards scientific and technological ideas. We aspire to establish this event as a leading global project competition, fostering a robust scientific temperament among the youth. This track is more than a competition; it's a platform for young innovators to make their mark on the world through their innovations. Let's turn your imagination into innovation!

The Objectives of the Junior Einstein Track

  • Global Showcase and Collaboration: Create an international platform for participants to showcase talents, innovations, and foster collaborations, laying the groundwork for future partnerships.
  • Next-Gen Innovator Recognition: Identify, recognize, and motivate the next generation of innovators to develop viable solutions for social issues through their creative ideas.
  • Societal Impact through STEM: Motivate students to leverage STEM concepts in developing products that address societal needs, contributing to the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals.
  • Hands-on Experience and Skill Development: Provide participants with hands-on experience in technology and engineering projects, fostering the development of critical thinking, problem-solving skills, and a passion for STEM fields.
  • Teamwork, Collaboration, and Innovation: Cultivate a culture of teamwork and collaboration while fostering creativity and innovation among participants, empowering them to think creatively and approach challenges collectively.

Who is eligible?

  • Junior high school students worldwide (12 – 17 year olds) who have a keen interest in Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM).
  • Passion for creativity and curiosity in STEM.
  • Desire to contribute to the world of STEM through innovative projects and problem-solving.

Benefits of participating

  • Global Recognition: Showcase your innovative ideas and stand a chance to be recognized on a global platform.
  • Skill Development: Hone critical thinking, problem-solving, and collaboration skills.
  • Networking: Connect with like-minded individuals, mentors, and professionals who share a passion for sustainability.
  • Awareness: Gain insights into pressing global issues and contribute to meaningful solutions.

Rules for participation

  • Team size: Maximum of 4 members.
  • Participant’s Age: 12 to 17 years. Proof for the same shall be submitted (Bonafide letter from school or birth certificate).
  • Team members may come from different academic institutions/industries and from different countries. Only the registered members of the team will be given certificates. Mentor names should also accompany the team registration.
  • Teams are locked along with abstract submission and no further changes to team members and mentors can be made thereafter.
  • The evaluation will be based on the following criteria: Novelty / Innovation, Participant’s knowledge on concept / market demand, Technical implementation, Expandability of the project idea/prototype to an actual product or solution, Clarity of pitch.
  • Plagiarism at any form or at any stage is strictly prohibited.
  • Each team is solely responsible for its own cooperation and teamwork.
  • The projects can be a software solution or hardware integrated.

Methodology

  • The participants will be evaluated in either of the two modes: Pilot entry mode or Direct entry mode.
  • Direct entry mode is for participants from countries without Pilots and will be evaluated based on their submission of Abstract, PPT, and Video demonstration of their project.
  • Pilot entry mode will select eligible teams for participation by conducting a preliminary project competition at their venue.
  • Teams need to submit the problem statement and the detailed solution as a PPT for evaluation by experts/Jury.
  • Selected teams will be notified and asked to submit an executive summary and video demonstration of their project.
  • During the finale, teams will present their projects virtually, evaluated by the jury.
  • Prizes will be awarded based on jury, track chair, and general chair recommendations.

Evaluation Criteria

  • Novelty & Innovation of the project idea.
  • Participant’s knowledge on the concept and market demand.
  • Technical implementation of the solution.
  • Demonstration and clarity of pitch.
  • Expandability of the project idea/prototype into an actual product or solution.

Selection Method

The selection process will be conducted in two main stages:

Stage 1 - Abstracts & Prelims

Abstract submission is open to all school students between ages 12 and 17 via Pilot entry or Direct Entry.

A) Direct entry participants will be evaluated based on abstract submission. Selected innovative projects will be informed via email.

B) Pilot entry participants present projects in a preliminary round conducted by pilots. Winners move to finals.

Stage 2 - Grand Finale

Selected teams will present their projects before the jury during the finals.

Evaluation will be carried out based on criteria such as novelty, technical implementation, clarity of pitch, and expandability of the idea.

FAQs:

1. How can I participate?
High school students aged 12-17 can participate by submitting their abstracts either via Pilots or Direct Entry. The selection process involves two stages: Abstracts scrutinization and Prelims, followed by the Grand Finale.
2. Can I participate as an individual, or do I need a team?
Teams of up to 4 members are welcome. You can form teams with students from different academic institutions or industries.
3. How will the projects be evaluated?
Projects will be evaluated based on novelty, clarity, feasibility, technical implementation, scalability, sustainability, and potential for future work progression.
4. Can projects be software-based or hardware-integrated?
Yes, projects can be either a software solution or hardware-integrated.
5. How is the Direct Entry mode evaluated in Stage 1?
Abstract submissions from Direct Entry participants are assessed, and innovative and creative projects are chosen for the finals.
6. What happens in Pilot Entry mode during Stage 1?
Participants in pilot entry mode showcase their projects in a preliminary round conducted by pilots. Selected teams advance to the finals.
7. Is plagiarism allowed during the selection process?
No, plagiarism at any stage is strictly prohibited.
8. What is the process after team selection?
Selected teams are required to submit an executive summary and a video demonstration of their project.
